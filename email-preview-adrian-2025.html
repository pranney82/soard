Meet Adrian — Our Little Miracle

Born prematurely at just 27 weeks during Hurricane Katrina, doctors said Adrian wouldn't survive an hour. He was a triplet, and heartbreakingly, his siblings passed away. Adrian fought through — and today, at 19, he talks nonstop, sings Taylor Swift, and calls his grandmother in Puerto Rico through Alexa.

Adrian lives with Cerebral Palsy and requires total physical care. His mom could only bathe him twice a week because of how dangerous and exhausting the old setup was — straddling the bathtub, lifting her 80+ lb son, balancing both their weight. There was no changing table, no wheelchair access, no room to maneuver safely.

That all changed.

"I want a new shower so Mommy doesn't have to step over the side."

— ADRIAN

What We Built Together

SOARD transformed Adrian's home with a wheelchair-accessible bathroom and a dream bedroom — designed with safety, independence, and joy at the center of every decision.

Accessible Bathroom

Roll-in shower with central showerhead, wheelchair-accessible sink for Adrian's independence, plenty of storage shared with his sister, and a layout redesigned for safe maneuvering.
 

Dream Bedroom

Custom bed designed to look like a shoebox, built-in closet and desk, a higher bed with safety rails, and a stair lift so Adrian can stay on the same floor as his family.
